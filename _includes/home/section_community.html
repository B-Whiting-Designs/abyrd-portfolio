Community

I believe that if public dollars supports my research then my research should be used to benefit community organizers and community learners. I volunteer in Kansas City to achieve this goal, from teaching workshops on race and racism to offering my own skills in writing and research to benefit charity partners. In addition, I link students with nonprofits for capacity-building projects! Listening to the needs of community partners, students create prototypes that community partners may or may not improve and use for later use. This approach maintains sustainable relationships between the university and the Metro Area and doesn't overburden already busy nonprofits! In the past, I've worked with Code for KC, Global FC, MARC, and Center for Neighborhoods! I would love to connect with others and learn how I and my students can serve you!

Re.Use.Full
When I moved to Kansas City, Missouri in August 2019, a friend introduced me to Code for KC, a chapter of Code for America comprised of "developers, designers, data geeks, policy buffs, and idea makers" that work with local government to make Kansas City a better place for all. I volunteer for for Re.Use.Full. This website is like Tinder: matches donors with lightly used goods to giveaway with charity partners that need them! In 2023, Re.Use.Full became a 503(c) nonprofit. Although I'm not longer an advisory board member, I ontinue to volunteer for events, like their wonderful UnDumpster Day!

Writing Counterstories about Race and Racism
In summer 2021 I taught a community course called Writing Counterstories about Race and Racism in Kansas City for Trinity United Methodist Church. In this six-week online course, a class of majority-White community learners first learned about what critical race theory and counterstories are. Then we brainstomed what race and racism looks like in the present and waht they looked like in the past. Having confronted White privilege and the limitations of liberal civil rights, students wrote and workshopped their own counterstories. The course concluded with naming actions the Church could committ to in the coming months.
